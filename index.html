About me, recent work.

Eden West is a collaborative pianist currently based in southeast Michigan. In the fall of 2026, she will begin her Master of Music in Collaborative Piano at The Juilliard School as a Kovner Fellow. A summa cum laude graduate of the University of Michigan, Eden holds dual bachelor’s degrees in Piano Performance and Music Education. Her training was guided by Logan Skelton and Ana María Otamendi, and her academic record earned her the distinction of being a nine-term James B. Angell Scholar and the recipient of the Presser Undergraduate Scholar Award.

West has began to build a professional portfolio in both vocal and instrumental realms. She recently served as a rehearsal pianist and coach for the Opera Company of Middlebury’s production of Trouble in Tahiti, performing both in the pit and on stage. During her time at the University of Michigan, she served as a staff pianist, where she music directed opera workshop scenes and collaborated with students and faculty for recitals, juries, and masterclasses. Additional training comes from Music Academy of the West, the Bayview Music Festival, the Collaborative Piano Institute, and SongFest.

Beyond her work at the piano, Eden is a certified educator and an active leader in the musical community. She served two terms as President of the University of Michigan’s chapter of the National Association for Music Education, and completed her student-teaching internship at Clague Middle School and Huron High School in Ann Arbor, MI. She also studied the violin for twelve years and continues to play in community orchestras.
