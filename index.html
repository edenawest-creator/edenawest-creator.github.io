Biography

About me,
my work,
and my passions.

Eden West is a collaborative pianist based in southeast Michigan. She holds degrees in Piano Performance and Music Education from the University of Michigan School of Music, Theatre & Dance, where she studied with Logan Skelton and Ana María Otamendi. During her undergraduate studies, she was recognized as a nine-term James B. Angell Scholar and was awarded both the 2024–25 Presser Undergraduate Scholar Award and the Virginia Allison Collaborative Award.

Training outside of her schooling include the Lehrer Vocal Institute at Music Academy of the West, the Collaborative Piano Institute, and SongFest.

At the University of Michigan, West works as a freelance pianist for the voice department. Her responsibilities include accompanying voice lessons, recitals, departmental performances, studio classes, voice literature classes, opera chorus rehearsals, and masterclasses.

Her experience as an educator includes completing her student-teaching assistantship at Huron High School with Timothy Krohn and Clague Middle School with Abigail Alwin.

West continues to work throughout southeast Michigan as a collaborative pianist, regularly partnering with singers, instrumentalists, dancers, and educators.
